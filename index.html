Colonial Narratives

For decades, the claim that homosexuality is "un‑African" has been repeated in political and cultural debates. However, scholars and activists argue that many of the laws criminalizing queer identities were introduced during European colonial rule. Understanding this history reveals how sexuality and power are intertwined with colonialism.

African Queer Voices

Kenya

The film Rafiki portrays a love story between two young women in Nairobi and challenges narratives that queer identities do not belong in African society.

South Africa

South Africa has one of the most progressive constitutions in the world regarding LGBTQ+ rights, while artists and scholars explore gender beyond Western categories.

Nigeria

Writers and artists use literature and storytelling to create spaces where queer African identities can exist despite legal and social challenges.

Imagining Freedom

African filmmakers, writers, and scholars imagine new futures where gender and sexuality are not restricted by colonial categories or rigid identities. Cultural production — film, literature, art, and music — becomes a powerful tool for expressing freedom and belonging.
